Introduction

Rashad Ramzan (S’04–M’09–SM’11) received the B.Sc. degree from the University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore, Pakistan, the M.Sc. degree from the Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm, Sweden, and the Ph.D. degree from Linkoping University, Linkoping, Sweden, in 1994, 2003, and 2009, respectively, all in electronics engineering. He has designed SMPS, low noise electronics, digital ASICS, RFICs, and cryogenically cooled electronics. He has been involved in research and development throughout his professional carrier. He has authored over 60 journals, US patents, and conference papers. One of his designed LNA was published in ISSCC 2007. His current research interests include software defined radio hardware, RFICs, DFT circuits for RFICs, LNAs, low power biomedical circuits, and microwave sensors and circuits. He was a recipient of the two Best Paper Awards. He is a Reviewer for several IEEE publications.

Research Interests:

RF Circuits, Signal Integrity Analysis, Analog & Mixed Circuit IC Design, VLSI Design, RF IC Design, Microwave Sensors, Energy Tunneling, Bio-Medical Electronics, and Low Power Circuits.
